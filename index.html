Key Projects

ROS2 Multirobot Task Allocation: Algorithmic Approaches
Embark on a journey to enhance multirobot coordination in ROS2 by investigating task allocation algorithms like Tessi, Genetic, RRT, and A*. Tessi facilitates decentralized task assignment, Genetic optimizes solutions, while RRT and A* ensure efficient path planning. Through rigorous experimentation, evaluate algorithmic performance and scalability in diverse environments. Discover how these approaches revolutionize multirobot systems by balancing task distribution and optimizing resource utilization. Gain insights into the strengths and limitations of each algorithm, paving the way for robust and adaptive task allocation strategies in complex real-world scenarios.
Baja SAE India 2023
Baja SAE involves engineering competitions mirroring real-life design projects and their obstacles. Students must create and construct an off-road vehicle capable of enduring tough terrain. Their objective is to develop a single-seat, all-terrain vehicle that's both durable and comfortable for the driver. The aim is to produce a prototype that's reliable, easy to maintain, user-friendly, and economically viable for recreational purposes 
Tilt-Rotor UAV Drone with the capability to climb on uneven surfaces through a sophisticated tilt-rotor mechanism
We utilized SolidWorks to design the model and performed thorough analyses with SimScale. This involved examining its structural strength using Finite Element Analysis (FEA) and airflow patterns with Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD). Currently, we're simulating the drone in ROS2 Gazebo.
e-Yantra: Engineering a better tomorrow 
In a team of three, we competed to navigate three holonomic drive robots using Gazebo (ROS) simulator localization. Our aim was to improve control realism, employing OpenCV for image processing and Aruco markers for robot localization. We also integrated inverse kinematics for finer control. This challenge showcased our expertise in robotics, image processing, and algorithm development, enhancing our skills in practical autonomous system applications.
