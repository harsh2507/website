Key Projects

Economics of PEM and Alkaline Electrolyzers: A Comparative Study for Hydrogen Production in Germany and India with Solar Integration
Projections reveal reduced hydrogen production costs by 2050—$0.52/kg for PEM electrolyzers and $0.68/kg for alkaline electrolyzers. My study quantified LCOE for Germany and India, showing strong cost efficiencies and economic potential. I also dissected PEM and alkaline electrolyzer costs, unveiling insights for stack deteriotaion and reduced CAPEX
Nuclear based Hydrogen Production : Economic Insights
Leveraging IAEA's Hydrogen Economic Evaluation Program (HEEP), we examined nuclear-powered hydrogen production by combining Pressurized Water Reactors (PWR) with conventional electrolysis. Calculating Levelized cost of Hydrogen, our results suggest economic feasibility in specific regions. South Korea leads at $2.74/kg, while US and India lag at $4.12/kg and $3.31/kg. Variations in overnight capital costs among nations influence the feasibility. 
Ammonia's Role in Hydrogen Transport: Financial Analysis
Explored ammonia's potential as a hydrogen carrier, uncovering estimated transportation costs of $6-8/kg for hydrogen via ships at varying distances. Analyzed cost drivers and exhaustively studied green ammonia transportation, revealing current shipping costs of $800-$950 per tonne of NH3, subject to distance. Projected an optimistic $200-$300 per tonne cost reduction ahead, underscoring the pathway to enhanced affordability.
Techno Economic Analysis of Hydrogen Production by Natural gas 
In our study, we discovered that a hydrogen plant employing Steam Methane Reforming (SMR) can achieve a cost of $2.05/kg without carbon sequestration and $3.33/kg with carbon sequestration in an Indian-based scenario. By employing sensitivity and Monte Carlo analysis techniques to examine key cost-influencing factors in hydrogen production, with feedstock consumption and cost emerging as primary determinants.
